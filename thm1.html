TryHackMe – Complete Beginner Path

Completing the Complete Beginner Path on TryHackMe was a pivotal step in shaping my professional goals. It served as an eye-opening introduction to the world of cybersecurity, a field i hadn’t had the chance to explore during my academic journey at EPHEC.

This hands-on training gave me the opportunity to test and acquire skills through a mix of theory and guided labs. The path was designed for beginners and required approximately 40 hours to complete, divided into clearly themed modules.

The content was strongly oriented towards web application security, covering key topics such as the OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities and the use of essential tools like Burp Suite (Proxy). In many ways, it felt like a natural extension of what i was learning at EPHEC, but with a much more practical and offensive security perspective.

The modules included:

  • Networking basics and fundamentals of the OSI model
  • Linux command line and privilege escalation
  • Enumeration techniques and service scanning
  • Web application security (XSS, SQLi, IDOR, etc.)
  • Credential attacks (bruteforce, password cracking)
  • Using tools like Nmap, Hydra, Gobuster, Burp Suite, etc.
  • Basic reverse engineering and scripting logic

While the content was not particularly difficult, it did require a significant investment of time and focus. My biggest strength was my curiosity and motivation to explore and understand how things work behind the scenes. However, my main challenge throughout the course was time management, staying consistent and balancing it with my academic workload wasn’t always easy.

In my opinion, this course helped me to get a feel for the field and gave me a preview of what lies ahead in my cybersecurity journey, i really enjoyed this as a starting point.
