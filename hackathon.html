Hackathon 2022 3rd edition

The Hackathon Ephec 2022 was the 3rd edition of the sustainable development themed Hackathon organized by the Bachelor's program in Computer Sciences at EPHEC. It took place during the last weekend of October 2022. The main theme of this year was "low-tech"

So what is the Hackathon? The Hackathon is an event of around 40 students divided into mixed teams. They had one mission: find a solution to a problem in 48H that integrate technicals skills, marketing and durable thinking.

The goal was to encourage innovation, interdisciplinary collaboration, and the practical applicaiton of the skills learned throughout their studies(which is the main philsophy of EPHEC)

During the hackathon, I had the opportunity to collaborate with Khika (to avoid mentioning her full name), Euan, Alassane, and Habibou. It was fascinating to observe how a project can be built from scratch in just 48 hours. The coaches of our teams were Lucas, Nathan and Alex, they are EPHEC alumni and they guided us throughout the project.

At the time, I was only in my first year of the Bachelor's program, but I learned a lot about web development. The coaches introduced me to modern frameworks like Angular, Vue.js, and React. Even though I didn't use them directly, they gave me a foundation to build later on.

I also learned how to handle web requests, which was a valuable step forward especially because understanding how the web works is essential in pentesting.

Our project was called Aidia, a palindrome (the name reads the same backward). The goal of Aidia was to provide a peer-to-peer support platform focused on everyday repair and assistance services. For example, if I’m a mechanic in real life and a neighbor posts a request on Aidia about a car issue, I can offer help directly — it's a simple way to connect people based on real skills and needs within their local community.

Working on Aidia was also very meaningful for my personal development as an aspiring web pentester. It gave me my first practical insight into how user data flows through a web application, how to handle requests, and form submissions — all of which are crucial to understanding modern attack surfaces and defending against them. I have also learned to work in a team with different type of profiles, it gaves me a chance to improve my networking skills.
