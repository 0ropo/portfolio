Youth Leader Training with Vacances Vivantes

I completed a basic youth leader training with Vacances Vivantes, and i found it to be an enriching experience.

The training taught me the fundamentals of social dynamics, both within a team of counselors and with children of all ages. At the end of the program, i was officially certified to work with kids aged 6 to 12.

However, for my very first camp, i was assigned to work with teenagers aged 14 to 18. It was really challenging, not only managing the energy of older kids, but also navigating team dynamics within the Youth leaders team which can be quite intense.

I quickly realized how crucial clear communication is: conveying ideas, taking initiative, and stepping out of my comfort zone were all essential skills that the training was emphasizing.

The experience was quite immersive. We barely slept, lived close together, and relied heavily on one another. But through the intensity, we build unforgettable memories and learned a lot about ourselves.

The training included both practical animation exercices(e.g, leading games, keeping participants engaged, clearly explaining rules) and deeper educational concepts, such as how to delegate responsability to a children.

Since i had never attended a youth camp myself, i had to rely purely on common sense and empathy.

For example: if something happens at the back of a group while we're walking. Do i stop everyone and run to the back alone? The answer is no, they are multiple solutions to this simple problem. Actually a good solution would be to assign a responsible child to lead the group while i handle the issue.

These kinds of situations taught me that before anything else, you need to act with humanity and intentions, and i found that incredibly meaningful
