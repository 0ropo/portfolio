About me

I'm Weili Zheng, currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Computer Sciences. I'm deeply passionate about cybersecurity and my professional goals are closely aligned with this interest and more specifically in the field of penetration testing. I still remember the moment that sparked it all: i was watching an interview with a professional pentester, and something just clicked. Since then, i'm commited to follow that path.

I'm naturally curious and constantly seeking new challenges in my daily life. I thrive in dynamic environments and tend to avoid repetitive routines. And that's the reason why the job of a pentester seems so appealing to me.

My strengths lies in my curiosity and strong desire to learn. However, one of the personal challenges i constantly work on is my tendency to be a bit absent-minded.

Discipline and attention to detail are areas where i'm still growing, but staying consistant and rigurous doesn't always come naturally to me, but i'm aware of it and actively improving each day.

From what i've learned, pentesters are sometimes called to carry out real-life simulations of cyberattacks even without the target compagny's awareness in order to assess their security. That means, beyond their technical skill in IT, web technologies and operating systems, they also need solid soft skills such as good communication, social engineering, and even lockpicking in some physical intrusion scenarios.
